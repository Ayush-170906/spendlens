# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Exact Target User

Engineering Manager or CTO at a seed-to-Series-A startup, 5-25 person team, paying for 3+ AI tools. They approved the tools individually over time and have never audited the combined spend. They feel vaguely guilty about the bill but have no benchmark.

## What they Google or scroll before wanting this tool

- "cursor vs github copilot which is better 2026"
- "chatgpt team plan worth it"
- "reduce AI tool costs startup"
- They scroll Hacker News Show HN threads about developer tools

## Where they hang out online

- Hacker News (Show HN, Ask HN)
- r/ExperiencedDevs, r/startups, r/SaaS
- Indie Hackers community
- Y Combinator Slack (alumni)
- Twitter/X — following @levelsio, @marc_louvion, @paulg

## First 100 users in 30 days with $0

1. **Post on Hacker News Show HN** — "Show HN: SpendLens — free AI spend audit for your team." HN is the highest-quality traffic for developer tools. One good Show HN can drive 500+ signups.
2. **Post in r/SaaS and r/startups** — "Built a free tool to audit your AI tool spend — found $400/mo in savings for my own team." Personal story format performs better than product announcements.
3. **Cold DM 50 CTOs on LinkedIn** — "I built a free tool that audits AI tool spend. Ran it on a 10-person team and found $380/mo in savings. Here's your free audit link." Include a real result.
4. **Tweet thread on X** — "Most startups are wasting $300+/month on AI tools without knowing it. Here's why [thread]" — end with SpendLens link.
5. **Post in Indie Hackers** — Document the build in public, share the tool as a milestone.
